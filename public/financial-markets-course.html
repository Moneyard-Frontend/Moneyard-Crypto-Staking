Moneyard Academy: Financial Markets Course

10-Part Beginner-Friendly Series

Lesson 1: Introduction to Financial Markets
What are financial markets and why are they important?
Financial markets are platforms where buyers and sellers trade financial instruments like stocks, bonds, and currencies. They enable efficient capital allocation, liquidity, and price discovery.
Lesson 2: Types of Financial Markets
Different categories and their functions.
Capital Markets: Long-term securities (stocks, bonds).
Money Markets: Short-term debt instruments.
Derivatives Markets: Futures, options based on underlying assets.
Forex Market: Currency trading.
Crypto Markets: Decentralized digital asset trading.
Lesson 3: Participants in Financial Markets
Who plays a role in financial transactions?
Includes retail investors, institutions, governments, brokers, dealers, and market makers. Each has a specific role in providing liquidity and stability.
Lesson 4: Primary vs Secondary Markets
How new and existing securities are traded.
Primary Market: New issues like IPOs.
Secondary Market: Resale of existing securities on exchanges.
Lesson 5: Stocks, Bonds, and Derivatives
Understanding core financial instruments.
Stocks: Equity ownership with potential dividends.
Bonds: Debt instruments with interest payments.
Derivatives: Contracts based on the value of underlying assets.
Lesson 6: Risk and Return
The tradeoff every investor must consider.
Higher returns usually involve higher risk. Understand market, credit, and interest rate risk. Diversification helps manage risk.
Lesson 7: Market Regulation
Maintaining fairness and transparency.
Regulated by bodies like SEC, FCA, and IOSCO to prevent fraud, protect investors, and ensure orderly markets.
Lesson 8: Behavioral Finance Basics
Why investors aren't always rational.
Common biases include overconfidence, herd behavior, and loss aversion. Behavioral finance helps explain anomalies in markets.
Lesson 9: Modern Trading & Crypto
Digital disruption in financial markets.
Mobile trading apps, algorithmic strategies, and decentralized exchanges like Uniswap and Binance Smart Chain are changing how markets function.
Lesson 10: Real-World Case Studies
Learning from past market events.
Dotcom Bubble: 1999-2001 tech crash.
2008 Crisis: Subprime mortgage collapse.
GameStop 2021: Retail investor-driven short squeeze.

BEGINNER Lesson 1/12

Market Participants

MARKET STRUCTURE │ ├── Retail Investors (35% of volume) │ ├── Individuals │ └── Small accounts │ ├── Institutional (60%) │ ├── Mutual/Pension Funds │ ├── Hedge Funds │ └── Insurance Companies │ └── Market Makers (5%) ├── Provide liquidity └── Profit from spreads
Key Insight: Institutional traders move markets but market makers ensure smooth trading.

Why This Matters:

Understanding who you're trading against helps anticipate price movements. Retail traders often follow institutional money flows.

Example: When Apple reports earnings, hedge funds react first, then mutual funds, and finally retail traders.

BEGINNER Lesson 2/12

Order Types Mastery

ORDER TYPE DECISION TREE │ ├── Need immediate execution? │ ├── Yes → MARKET ORDER │ └── No → Continue │ ├── Want price control? │ ├── Yes → LIMIT ORDER │ │ ├── Buy: ≤ target │ │ └── Sell: ≥ target │ └── No → Continue │ └── Need protection? ├── Stop-LOSS (limit downside) └── Stop-BUY (enter breakouts)
Golden Rule: Market orders fill fast but unpredictably, limit orders control price but may not fill.

Practical Tips:

  • Use limit orders in volatile markets
  • Market orders work best for liquid stocks
  • Stop orders become market orders when triggered

Stocks vs Bonds: Core Differences

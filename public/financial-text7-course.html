Market Participants

Core Concept

Financial markets are driven by different types of participants, each with distinct roles and behaviors.

MARKET ECOSYSTEM │ ├── Retail Investors (Individuals) │ ├── Trade through brokers │ └── Typically <$1M accounts │ ├── Institutional Investors │ ├── Mutual Funds ($100M+) │ ├── Pension Funds ($B+) │ └── Hedge Funds (Sophisticated) │ └── Market Makers ├── Provide liquidity └── Profit from bid-ask spreads

Futures Contracts Deep Dive

Core Concept

Futures are standardized contracts to buy/sell assets at future dates, used for hedging and speculation with unique leverage characteristics.

FUTURES MECHANICS │ ├── CONTRACT SPECS │ ├── Underlying asset │ ├── Contract size │ ├── Expiration date │ └── Tick size/value │ ├── MARGIN REQUIREMENTS │ ├── Initial margin │ └── Maintenance margin │ └── DAILY SETTLEMENT ├── Mark-to-market └── Margin calls if equity ↓

Example: E-mini S&P 500 Futures

  • Contract Size: $50 × S&P 500 index
  • Margin: ~$12,000 per contract
  • Daily Move: 1pt = $50 profit/loss
  • Leverage: 20:1 (index at 5,000 → $250k notional)

Behavioral Finance Basics

Core Concept

Psychological factors often cause investors to make irrational decisions that contradict traditional finance theory.

COMMON COGNITIVE BIASES │ ├── Loss Aversion │ ├── Pain of loss > joy of gain │ └── Leads to holding losers too long │ ├── Herding │ ├── Following the crowd │ └── Buying high, selling low │ └── Anchoring ├── Fixating on purchase price └── Reluctance to sell below "anchor"

Real-World Examples

Dot-com bubble: Herding into overvalued tech stocks

2008 crisis: Anchoring to pre-crash home prices

COVID crash: Loss aversion causing panic selling

Volatility Smiles & Surfaces

Core Concept

The volatility smile shows how implied volatility varies with strike price, revealing market expectations and arbitrage opportunities.

VOLATILITY SURFACE /\ / \ ↗ Term structure / \ / ___/ \__/ ITM ATM OTM SPX Options Typical Pattern: │ ├── OTM puts: High vol (crash fear) ├── ATM: Normal vol └── OTM calls: Moderate vol

Quantitative Implications

  1. Black-Scholes Failure: Assumes constant vol across strikes
  2. Skew Trading: Buy cheap wings vs. sell expensive ATM
  3. VIX Calculation: Weighted average of near-term options

Volatility Analysis

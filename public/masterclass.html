Personal Finance Course

A structured guide to mastering money from beginner to advanced level.

Beginner Level

Lesson 1: Understanding Money

Topics: What is money? Income vs. Expenses.

Example: If you earn $1,000/month and spend $800, you save $200.

Lesson 2: Setting Financial Goals

Topics: Short, medium, long-term goals.

Example: Save for a phone, car, or home.

Lesson 3: Budgeting Basics

Topics: 50/30/20 rule.

Example: On $1,000: $500 needs, $300 wants, $200 savings.

Lesson 4: Emergency Fund

Topics: Safety net for life’s surprises.

Example: Save 3–6 months of expenses ($2,400–$4,800).

Lesson 5: Introduction to Debt

Topics: Good vs. bad debt.

Example: Student loans vs. high-interest credit cards.

Lesson 6: Bank Accounts

Topics: Checking vs. savings.

Example: Use checking for spending, savings for goals.

Lesson 7: Financial Discipline

Topics: Needs vs. wants, delayed gratification.

Example: Skip coffee shop 3x/week = $60 saved/month.

Lesson 8: Avoiding Scams

Topics: Red flags, online fraud.

Example: Don't share banking details or OTPs.

Lesson 9: Introduction to Insurance

Topics: Health, auto, life basics.

Example: A $100/month premium can save $10,000 in hospital bills.

Lesson 10: Financial Mindset

Topics: Abundance vs. scarcity mindset.

Example: Believing “I can learn to grow wealth” helps financial growth.

Intermediate Level

Lesson 11: Saving Strategies

Topics: Automation, buckets.

Example: Automatically transfer $100 to savings monthly.

Lesson 12: Debt Repayment Plans

Topics: Snowball vs. Avalanche.

Example: Prioritize smallest or highest interest first.

Lesson 13: Credit Score & Reports

Topics: How to build credit.

Example: Use <30% of credit limit, always pay on time.

Lesson 14: Basics of Investing

Topics: Risk, reward, stocks.

Example: $100/month in stocks at 8% = ~$15,000 in 10 years.

Lesson 15: Retirement Planning

Topics: Start early, compound interest.

Example: $200/month from age 25 = $620,000 at 65 (8% return).

Lesson 16: Health Insurance Essentials

Topics: Premium, deductible, coverage.

Example: Know what services are included before buying a policy.

Lesson 17: Avoiding Lifestyle Inflation

Topics: Spending rises with income.

Example: Keep your old car even with a raise = more savings.

Lesson 18: Saving for Children/Education

Topics: 529 plans, trusts.

Example: Saving $50/month for 18 years = college help fund.

Lesson 19: Investment Accounts

Topics: Brokerage, Roth IRA, 401(k).

Example: Roth IRA = tax-free growth if used in retirement.

Lesson 20: Understanding Inflation

Topics: Why prices rise, real returns.

Example: $1 today may only buy $0.85 worth of goods in 5 years.

Advanced Level

Lesson 21: Tax Planning

Topics: Deductions, credits.

Example: Max out IRA to reduce taxable income.

Lesson 22: Asset Allocation

Topics: Diversifying across asset classes.

Example: 70% stocks, 20% bonds, 10% cash.

Lesson 23: Real Estate Investing

Topics: Buying, renting, equity.

Example: Buy $200k home, rent for $1,200/month.

Lesson 24: Entrepreneurship & Side Hustles

Topics: Freelance, online business.

Example: Start a digital store or content channel.

Lesson 25: Estate Planning

Topics: Wills, trusts, beneficiaries.

Example: Use a living trust to avoid probate.

Lesson 26: Advanced Credit Strategies

Topics: Rewards, utilization hacks.

Example: Use cards for bills, pay in full = rewards + credit boost.

Lesson 27: Passive Income

Topics: Dividends, royalties, rentals.

Example: Invest in dividend-paying ETFs.

Lesson 28: Risk Management

Topics: Hedging, diversification, insurance.

Example: Don't invest all in one sector.

Lesson 29: FIRE Movement

Topics: Financial Independence, Retire Early.

Example: Save 50%+ of income, retire by 40.

Lesson 30: Cryptocurrency Basics

Topics: Bitcoin, wallets, risks.

Example: Understand volatility before investing.

Lesson 31: Generational Wealth

Topics: Building legacy, passing wealth.

Example: Buy assets that appreciate, teach kids money skills.

Lesson 32: Philanthropy & Giving

Topics: Donations, social impact.

Example: Give 5% of income to causes that matter to you.
